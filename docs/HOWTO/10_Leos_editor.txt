    
                            LEOS EDITOR - HOWTO
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
								INTRODUCTION

The editors features available for each components are defined based on the
Commission business rules. Some elements might or not be available based on
theses rules.

                             SAVING YOUR WORK
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.	3 buttons are available at the top left of the editor:
	- Save - will save your changes without closing the editing block
	- Save Close - will save your changes and close the editing block
	- Close - will cancel your changes and close the editing block


10a.                         CITATIONS EDITION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  On mouse-over citations in the document view, the action buttons will be
    visible on the right side of the highlighted citations.
   
2.  Double-click on the citations content or click on the action button with a  
    pen icon to edit the citations. Citations in the document will become 
    available for editing. Editable portion of the document will be highlighted.
   
3.  The editable area will be accompanied by of a floating tool-bar specially 
    customised for citations with different action buttons.

4.  To create a new citation before or after an existing citation, place 
	the cursor on this citation and from the action buttons on the right side 
	select 'Insert citation before' OR 'Insert citation after'. A new citation 
	will be created.
	
5.  To delete an existing citation place the cursor on this citation and from  
	the action buttons on the right side click on 'Delete citation'. 
    The citation will be deleted.
   

10b.                        RECITALS EDITION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  On mouse-over recitals in the document view, the action buttons will be
    visible on the right side of the highlighted recitals.
   
2.  Double-click on the recitals content or click on the action button with a  
    pen icon to edit the recitals. Recitals in the document will be available for 
    editing. Editable portion of the document will be highlighted.
   
3.  The editable area will be accompanied by of a floating tool-bar specially 
    customised for recitals with different action buttons.

4.  To create a new recital before or after an existing recital, place the cursor
	on a existing recital and and from the action buttons select 
	'Insert recital before' OR 'Insert recital after'.
	A new recital will be created with the number auto-generated.
  
5.  To delete an existing recital place the cursor on this recital and from  
	the action buttons on the right side click on 'Delete recital'. 
    The recital will be deleted.



10c.                        ARTICLE EDITION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  On mouse-over the article in the document view, the action buttons will be
    visible on the right side of the highlighted article.
   
2.  Double-click on the article content or click on the action button with a  
    pen icon to edit the article. The article will become available for editing. 
    Editable portion of the document will be highlighted.
   
3.  The editable area will be accompanied by of a floating tool-bar specially 
    customised for articles with different action buttons.

4.  To create a new article before or after an existing article, place the cursor
	on a existing article and and from the action buttons select 
	'Insert article before' OR 'Insert article after'.
	A new article will be created with the number auto-generated.
  
5.  To delete an existing article place the cursor on this article and from  
	the action buttons on the right side click on 'Delete article'. 
    The article will be deleted.
   
   
10d.                            NUMBERED PARAGRAPH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  To create a new numbered paragraph place the cursor at the desired position
    (start, end, in between) of the existing numbered paragraph and press the
    'ENTER' key on the keyboard. A new numbered paragraph will be created with
    the number auto-generated.
   
2.  To merge an existing numbered paragraph with the previous numbered paragraph
    place the cursor at the beginning of the numbered paragraph and press
    'BACKSPACE' key on the keyboard.
   
3.  To merge an existing numbered paragraph with the next numbered paragraph
    place the cursor at the end of the numbered paragraph and press 'DELETE' key
    on the keyboard.
   
4.  To delete an existing numbered paragraph select the entire numbered 
    paragraph text and press 'DELETE' or 'BACKSPACE' key on the keyboard.
	
5. To switch form numbered paragraph to unumbered paragraph, click on the
   action button marked as 'paragraph mode' available in the Leos editor tool-bar.

	

10e.                             SUB-PARAGRAPH
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  To create a sub-paragraph place the cursor at the desired position(start,
    end, in between) of the existing numbered paragraph and press 
    'SHIFT + ENTER' keys on the keyboard.

2.  The content of the numbered paragraph will be transformed into sub-paragraph
    and a new sub-paragraph is inserted in the new line available for editing.

3.  To create subsequent sub-paragraphs press 'SHIFT + ENTER', a new 
    sub-paragraph will be inserted in the new line.
   
4.  To delete an existing sub-paragraph select the entire sub-paragraph content 
    and press 'DELETE' or 'BACKSPACE' key on the keyboard.
   

10f.            TRANSFORM NUMBERED PARAGRAPH TO POINT AND VICE-VERSA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  To transform numbered paragraph into a point place the cursor anywhere in 
    the existing paragraph and click on the tool-bar button with the label 
    'Increase Indent'.

2.  The paragraph will be transformed into a point and all its children will be
    the child of the new point.
	
3.  To create subsequent points place the cursor at the desired position(start,
    end, in between) of the existing point and press the 'ENTER' key button on
    the keyboard. The point numbers are auto-generated.

4.	You can use 'Increase Indent' to create an ordered list up to four levels as
	shown below:

5.  The first numbered paragraph in the article cannot be transformed to point
    and the tool-bar button for indentation will be disabled.

6.  Point can be transformed into numbered paragraph using the 'Decrease Indent'
    tool-bar button wherever possible.
	



10g.                             REFERENCES NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  To create an reference note place the cursor at the desired position in the
    edited area and click the tool-bar button with the label 'Insert footnote'.
   
2.  A dialog will open with an input field available to write the reference
    note text. Click 'Ok' button to close the dialog.
   
3.  A new reference note will be created with the number auto-generated and the 
    numbering of the remaining references notes will be adjusted accordingly.
   
3.  To edit an existing reference note double-click on it. A dialog will 
    appear to edit the reference note text. Edit the existing text and click 
    'Ok'
   
4.  To delete an existing reference note select it with the mouse and press the
    'DELETE' key on the keyboard. The reference note will be deleted and the
    numbering of the remaining references notes will be adjusted accordingly.
   
5.  To 'drag n drop' an existing reference note select it from the little 
    square icon that appears over it and drag it to the desired position then 
    drop it. The numbering of all the existing references notes will adjust 
    accordingly.  


10h.                             CROSS-REFERENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  To create a cross-reference to any element inside the current document, open
    the editor and place the cursor at the desired location and click the
    tool-bar button with the label 'Internal reference'.

2.  A dialog will open with the title 'Cross-reference'. The left hand side is 
    the document tree and the right hand side is the content area which shows 
    the content of the selected element on the tree.

3.  Expand the tree and select the element from Preamble(Citations, Recitals) or
    Enacting terms(Article) to display it's content on the content area.

4.  It is possible to select the element within the content for e.g. citation,
    recital, paragraph, sub-paragraph, point etc to make it as a cross-reference
    .The selected element is labelled using the input field 'Show reference
    as' provided at the bottom of the dialog window.
    The selected element and the reference label are mandatory to create a cross
    -reference.
   
5.  Click 'Ok' to close the dialog. The cross-reference  label will be inserted
    at the position of the cursor. Click 'Save' to save the editor content.
   
6.  To edit an existing cross-reference double-click on the reference label, it 
    will open the cross-reference dialog with the selected element highlighted.
    A different element can be selected to modify the referenced element or the
    reference label value can be edited.

7.  To remove an existing cross-reference select it with the mouse and press the
    'DELETE' key on the keyboard.

8.  To 'drag n drop' an existing cross-reference select it from the little 
    square icon that appears over it and drag it to the desired position then 
    drop it.
	
   

10i.                                   TABLES
 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. To create a table place the cursor at the desired position in the
    edited area and click the tool-bar button with the label 'Table'.
   
2.  A dialog will open with input fields available to define the table properties.
    Click 'Ok' button to close the dialog.
   
3.  A new table will be created with the defined properties (rows/columns).
   
4.  To fill it in, just place your mouse cursor in the desired cell and type your
	content.

5.  To edit the table structure or properties, simply right click over the table
	and use the contextual menu.
   
6.  To delete an existing the full table, right click over and choose "Delete Table"

   
10j.                                 FORMULAS
 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. To create a formula place the cursor at the desired position in the
    edited area and click the tool-bar button with the label 'Math'.
   
2.  A dialog will open with an input field  to define the formula using
	LaTeX and a preview. 
    Click 'Ok' button to close the dialog.
   
3.  The formulas will be inserted at the cursor position.
   
4.  To edit the formula, double click on it
   
5.  To delete a formula  select it with the mouse and press either the 'DELETE' or 
	the 'BACKSPACE' key on the keyboard.
	
   
10k.                                INSERT SPECIAL CHARACTER
 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. To insert a special character place the cursor at the desired position in the
    edited area and click the tool-bar button with the label 'Insert Special Character'.
   
2.  A dialog will open with a set of special character to choose from to the left and
    to the right a preview of that character in normal and caps.
	Click on the special character you need to insert it and close the dialog.
   
3.  To replace it, just delete it and insert the correct one.   
   
4.  To delete a special character, select it with the mouse and press
    either the 'DELETE' or the 'BACKSPACE' key on the keyboard.
	

	
10l.								SHOW SEMANTICS BLOCKS
 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------								

1. In order to show the semantic behinds the editor, click the tool-bar button with
   the label 'Show blocks'
   
2. Some blocks with the semantic label in the background of your text.

3. To hide it, just click again on the 'Show blocks' button.


10m.								SHOW AKN CODE
 --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 
 1. This button is only available for users having support role
 
 2. To show the AKN source, click on the tool-bar button with the label 'Source'.
 
 3. A dialog will open showing the AKN code of that edition block.
 
 4. Clicking on 'OK' will save any change you would have made in the code and close the dialog.
 
 5. Clicking on 'Cancel' will close the dialog (without saving)
